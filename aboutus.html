History

Royal Ballroom is a ballroom dancing school for people aged over 75. It was founded in 2007 by Ada Wilson. Her grandmother, an award winning ballroom dancer, injured her hip and her mobility became limited. Ada wanted her grandmother to be able to continue dancing. Since she could not find a place that would cater to her needs, she decided to start Royal Ballroom to help not only her grandmother, but anyone over 75 who wants to dance. The name Royal Ballroom came from her grandmother, who always said she felt like a princess when dancing.

What We Do

Royal Ballroom provides classes for people with all levels all experience with ballroom dancing: from no experience to experts. Our team of instructors takes into account the needs and capacity of each student. Whether you have perfect health, a bad knee, or prefer to sit down, we will adapt the training to accommodate it.
